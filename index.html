Where am I now?

I'm a Computer Science Masters student at Brigham Young University. I've been here a while (I began my BS in 2008) and am planning to stay for a PhD. So I'll be here a while yet.

I've been doing research related to computer assisted transcription of historical documents. My thesis is focused on an application of word spotting to transcription, particularly computer assisted transcription.

I grew up in the Seattle, WA area until I came to Utah for college. I started at BYU in 2008 and left on a volunteer two-year, full-time mission for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints. After returning, I finished my BS in Computer Science in 2013. I met my beautiful wife whom I married immediately following our graduation. We now have a wonderful little daughter.

My favorite thing to do is hike in the mountains around Provo and on the red rocks of southern Utah. My favorite sport is Ultimate Frisbee. I love to play games of all types. I got interested in programming after fiddling with a game making program in junior high, and then took the AP CS classes offered in highschool.
